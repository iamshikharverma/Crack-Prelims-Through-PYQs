# Crack UPSC - A PYQ Practice Platform

Hi there! I'm a UPSC aspirant, and I built this platform to help myself and fellow aspirants master the Prelims through a more strategic, question-first approach. 

This is an open project, and I welcome contributions from the community—whether it's adding more questions, improving the UI, or refining the practice algorithms.

## The Philosophy: The "BAWA" Approach

The core philosophy of this project is to flip the traditional preparation model on its head. Instead of getting lost in the "endless syllabus," we focus on learning concepts through questions.

### The Strategy
- **Learn through PYQs**: We host around 3,000 Previous Year Questions (PYQs) dating back to 1980. These questions cover almost every subject asked in the UPSC Prelims.
- **Problem Solving over Rote Learning**: UPSC isn't just a knowledge test; it's a test of problem-solving skills. Can you break down a question? Can you eliminate options smartly? Can you perform under pressure?
- **Rehearsed Performance**: Think of the Prelims less like a knowledge test and more like a strategy game. By getting comfortable with PYQs, you are training for the actual "performance" on exam day.

> "Enjoy preparing for UPSC like you enjoy playing computer games, because the winner often is the one who has the most fun playing it – that’s the 'BAWA' approach. Not just for exams, but honestly, for life too."

## Features
- **Active Recall & Spaced Repetition**: Integrated SRS (Anki-style) to help you focus on what you find "Hard."
- **Topic-wise Filtering**: Drill down into specific sub-topics within subjects.
- **Wrong Only Mode**: Automatically track and review questions you've struggled with.
- **Historical Data**: Practice with questions from the last 30+ years.
